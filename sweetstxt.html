Atreyapuram Putharekulu

What is Jaggery Dry Fruits Putharekulu?

Jaggery dry fruits Putharekulu is a delightful and nutritious traditional sweet treat originating from the southern regions of India, particularly Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. This mouthwatering delicacy combines the goodness of wholesome jaggery with the crunch and richness of various dry fruits, creating a perfect balance of flavors and textures. Jaggery Putharekulu offers a unique and irresistible taste experience. With its caramel-like notes, the natural sweetness of jaggery perfectly complements the nutty and fruity flavors of the dry fruits. This combination tantalizes the taste buds and provides a nutritious indulgence rich in vitamins, minerals, and healthy fats. The origins of putharekulu can be traced back to ancient times, when jaggery, a natural sweetener made from the concentrated sap of various palm trees, was widely used in Indian sweets and desserts. Putharekulu, which translates to "sweet sheet rolls" in Telugu, emerged as a beloved delicacy in the households of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana, where the art of crafting these sweet morsels was passed down through generations.

Due to their nutritious ingredients, jaggery dry fruits Putharekulu offer many health benefits. The iron content in jaggery helps combat anaemia and improve haemoglobin levels, while calcium strengthens bones and teeth. The dry fruits incorporated in these delectable treats are powerhouses of nutrients themselves. Cashews, almonds, and pistachios are excellent sources of healthy fats, plant-based proteins, and various vitamins and minerals. These dry fruits also contain antioxidants, which help neutralise free radicals and protect the body from oxidative stress. Jaggery Putharekulu is a great dietary fibre source, promoting healthy digestion and regulating bowel movements. The combination of jaggery and dry fruits provides a slow release of energy, making these treats an excellent snack option for sustained energy levels throughout the day. Moreover, the natural sweetness of jaggery in this Putharekulu can help curb cravings for unhealthy sugary treats, making them a guilt-free indulgence for those watching their calorie intake or managing diabetes.

What is Bandar Laddu?

Bandaru (Tokkudu) Laddu Tuesday, November 6th, 2018 by Ujwala Leave a Comment Bandaru Laddu Bandaru Laddu is also known as Thokudu Laddu – one of Andhra’s most favorite sweet. They are originated in a place called “Machilipatnam” in the state of Andhra Pradesh, India. The so-called modern day Machilipatnam used to be called “Bandaru”. Hence the name Bandaru Laddu. These laddus made by pounding the fried besan murukus. In the Telugu language which is spoken in the state of Andhra Pradesh Pounding is called tokkudu. Hence the name Tokkudu Laddu. Bandaru laddu, with its striking and smooth velvety texture melt right in your mouth. You are lucky to find these laddus in sweet stalls. Only until recent years these are only made at home, never in sweet stalls. In addition to its delectable taste, bandaru laddu will wow you with its long shelf life. Bandaru laddu will stay fresh for three to four weeks, which can be extended by refrigerating and avoiding milk in the recipe. The right creamy melt in the mouth texture comes from pounding the mix in an appropriate stone pestle. Though there are many cut short methods to make these, they don’t yield good results. This is a recipe i got from my elders in the family which has been followed for more than 2 decades and are prepared during weddings and other auspicious occasions.

What are the Ingredients for Bandar Laddu?

300 grams of besan / chickpea flour / senaga pindi (best quality that smells fresh) 300 grams of grated white jaggery (jaggery that is light in color) (2 cups grated) ½ cup water for jaggery syrup ½ cup sugar ¼ tsp. cardamom powder 1 cup solid ghee (if needed you will have to use few tbsps. extra to 1/4 cup) Fistful of cashews and raisins (I did not use raisins) Rock sugar small pieces (I did not use)
