Ponduru khadi

What is Ponduru Khadi?

Ponduru Khadi is a special variety of handspun and handwoven fabric, made in the town of Ponduru in Srikakulam district, Andhra Pradesh, India. It's renowned for its high quality, fine texture, and the traditional spinning methods used in its production. The fabric is made from cotton, spun into yarn using a traditional charkha (spinning wheel), and then woven into cloth by skilled artisans. Ponduru Khadi holds cultural and historical significance, as it is closely associated with the Indian independence movement, particularly Mahatma Gandhi, who admired this fabric. Today, it remains a symbol of craftsmanship and sustainability, often used for clothing, sarees, and garments that promote eco-friendly fashion.

Uppada Jamdani Sarees

What is uppada Jamdani Saree?

The Uppada Jamdani silk saree dates back to the 18th century when Jamdani, a Persian word, denoting an art of weaving that originated in Bengal, was revived and introduced to weavers in the twin coastal villages of Uppada-Kothapalli in the Kakinada district of Andhra Pradesh. The saree, also coined as Andhra Uppada is inspired by the Jamdani tradition but maintains a distinct local character resulting in acquisition of a GI tag in 2009, with paisley, floral and geometric motifs woven on the finest of pure silk. Cotton is used for both Warp and Weft and Zari for Extra Warp & Extra Weft. Threads are interlaced to form the designs and no wonder, the magnificence of the saree shines through every thread.
